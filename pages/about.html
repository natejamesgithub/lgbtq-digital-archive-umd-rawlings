About the D.C. LGBTQ+ Digital Archive

The D.C. LGBTQ+ Digital Archive is a community-centered action proposal created for the Rawlings Undergraduate Leadership Fellows Program at the University of Maryland School of Public Policy.

Our Proposal

This project responds to the need for accessible, community-driven preservation of LGBTQ+ stories, visuals, oral histories, and cultural memory across Washington, D.C. and the surrounding DMV region.

Rather than treating history as something locked away in institutions, this archive imagines a public-facing digital space where community members, students, organizers, artists, and elders can contribute, browse, and learn from local LGBTQ+ experiences.

As a Rawlings Fellowship community action proposal, our goal is to connect public policy, storytelling, digital access, and local memory. The archive is designed to support visibility, education, and intergenerational connection while making contribution simple for non-technical users.
